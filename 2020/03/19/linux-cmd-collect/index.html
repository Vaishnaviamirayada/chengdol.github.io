Linux Commands Collect

Posted on 2020-03-19 | Edited on 2020-04-29 | In Linux

Frequently used Linux commands collected and sorted by first letter:

a

awk (data processing and extracting), alias, at (schedule job one time), arp (check layer2 to layer3 mapping)

b

base64 (encode/decode), bg, basename (last name), blkid

c

cat, cd, cp, cut ,curl ,chown ,chmod ,chgrp ,cron ,clear

d

df (disk space check), dirname (path prefix), du (occupied space check), diff, date, dd (convert and copy), dnsdomainname, dig (dns lookup utility)

e

echo, exit, export, env, exportfs, ethtool (physical card)

f

file, free, find, fg, firewall-cmd, fallocate (fast allocate space)

g

gzip, grep, git, gitk, getfacl, getent(look up /etc/hosts)

h

hostname, host, htpasswd, history, hostnamectl (permenant hostname)

i

ip (so powerful), ifconfig (obsolete), id, iperf3, iptables, iostat, ifdown, ifup

j

jq, jobs, journalctl

k

kill

l

ls, less, ln, lsblk, lvdisplay, lscpu, lastlog, last (reboot)

m

mv, mount, more, man, mkdir, mktemp

n

nc, netstat, nslookup, nmap, nice (process priority)

o

openssl

p

pwd, ping, ps, perf, pvdisplay, pmap, pwdx

q

so far no!

r

rm, rmdir, route, rsync, readlink, runlevel, renice, rpm

s

ssh, scp, sftp, strace, sudo, su, sed (stream editor), setfacl,
sleep, stat, systemctl, shutdown, sar (system activity report), ss(similar to netstat)

t

tar, tcpdump (wireshark), top (keep update), trap, touch, tee, tail, tree, tracepath, traceroute, tload, tc (traffic control)

u

uniq, uname, umount, unlink, uuidgen, uptime

v

vim, vmstat

w

w, who, wget, wc, wall, write, watch

x

xargs

y

yum

z

zip
